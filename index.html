From Biology to Browsers, Meet Alex Brady

After four years working in the tech space, I decided it was time to flip the script—swapping recruitment calls for console.log() and career coaching for clean code. I have a Master’s in Biology from the University of Sheffield and a curiosity that just won’t quit, I’m diving straight into HTML, CSS, and JavaScript via _Nology. Now, instead of just talking to great developers I’m becoming one

While I’m new to web development, I’m not new to solving problems, communicating clearly, and getting things done. Here’s what I’m bringing with me:

  • People skills, perfected. Four years in technical recruitment sharpened my ability to communicate with all kinds of people—developers, designers, stakeholders—and translate technical needs into real-world solutions
  • Analytical mindset. My background in Biology means I’ve spent a lot of time analyzing complex systems. Debugging code? Just a different kind of organism to dissect.
  • Project management instincts. From managing hiring pipelines to juggling client expectations, I’ve learned how to stay organized, prioritize tasks, and hit deadlines (without losing my mind).
  • Curiosity + adaptability. Whether it’s genomics or JavaScript, I love digging into new topics and learning fast. I don’t mind being the beginner—because I won’t be one for long.

I'm not just learning how to build websites—I'm building a new career on a strong foundation of skills I already know work.
